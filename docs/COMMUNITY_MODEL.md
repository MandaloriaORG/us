# Community Model

## Identity

Mandaloria should feel like a community with its own structure.

Identity is composed of:

- users;
- profiles;
- clans or houses;
- ranks;
- roles;
- badges;
- reputation;
- contributions;
- friends or connections.

## Users

Each user has:

- authentication account;
- public profile;
- display name;
- avatar;
- biography;
- optional clan/house;
- community rank;
- permission roles;
- join date;
- contribution history;
- friends or connections visible according to privacy.

## Roles

Roles control permissions.

Initial roles:

- User: can post, comment, and react.
- Verified member: user with community trust.
- Archivist: can create or edit library content.
- Moderator: can review reports and moderate content.
- Guardian: senior moderator or high-trust role.
- Administrator: controls configuration, users, and structure.

A user can have multiple roles.

## Ranks

Ranks represent progression or community status. They should not always grant technical permissions.

Possible initial ranks:

- Initiate
- Member
- Companion
- Mentor
- Guardian
- Founder

Ranks may depend on:

- seniority;
- contributions;
- reputation;
- badges;
- manual decision by administrators.

## Clans and Houses

Clans/houses group members by identity, affinity, or internal structure. They should not just be decorative labels: they have a concrete responsibility within the community.

Each clan/house has:

- name;
- slug;
- description;
- emblem;
- color;
- leader;
- members;
- optional internal rules;
- mission and areas of responsibility;
- own spaces and channels according to permissions;
- relationship with sections or Codex topics it helps maintain;
- status: active, private, archived.

A House can:

- care for specific areas of the Codex without becoming its owner;
- organize research expeditions;
- propose conversations or findings for the Codex;
- recognize contributions with verifiable badges if it has authority;
- elect internal leaders;
- publish announcements and maintain memory of its decisions.

An expedition has objective, leaders, participants, sources, status, and result. Its result can end in a post, a knowledge proposal, or a Codex article.

Possible types:

- House: large or historical group.
- Clan: social or activity group.
- Circle: study or specific-topic group.

## Clan/House Membership

A user can:

- belong to no clan;
- belong to a main clan/house;
- have secondary memberships in circles;
- have an internal role within the clan/house.

Possible internal roles:

- member;
- veteran;
- mentor;
- leader;
- founder.

## Badges

Badges recognize achievements or contributions.

Examples:

- Founder
- First oath
- Archivist
- Mentor
- Featured contribution
- Builder of Mandaloria
- Guardian of the community

Badges can be:

- automatic;
- manual;
- temporary;
- exclusive.

A verifiable badge shows:

- who issued it;
- when it was issued;
- reason;
- related evidence or contribution;
- current status: active or revoked.

Revoking a badge hides it or marks it as revoked according to policy, but does not erase its internal history. A badge does not grant permissions unless an explicit independent rule exists.

## Reputation

Reputation measures useful contributions, not empty popularity.

Possible sources:

- featured posts;
- useful comments;
- articles accepted into the library;
- positive reactions;
- mentor actions;
- moderate and constructive participation.

Reputation should not replace moderator judgment.

## Friends and Connections

Connections help Mandaloria be a community network and not just a list of posts.

First version:

- friend requests;
- accept or reject requests;
- remove friendship;
- block user;
- view friends on profile if privacy allows.

Future version:

- follow users without friendship;
- privacy by relationship;
- clan/house invitations;
- circles of trust.

## Initial Rules

The community must have simple rules from the start:

- respect among members;
- debates with arguments;
- no spam;
- no harassment;
- no impersonation;
- no illegal content;
- follow moderation decisions;
- care for the philosophy and shared knowledge.
